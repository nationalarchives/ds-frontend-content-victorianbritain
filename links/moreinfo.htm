POWYS DIGITAL HISTORY PROJECT:

Victorian Powys For Key Stage 2

Now, with funding from the Heritage Lottery Fund and Powys County Council's Education Department via GEST, a new project started in April 2000 to portray the changes and developments that took place across Powys during the Victorian period. This project will specifically address the requirements of Key Stage 2 under the Cwricwlwm Cymraeg and can be found at http://history.powys.org.uk/school1.

The emphasis will be on the interpretation of materials, using text aimed specifically at the 9-11 age range and specially commissioned drawings. The project will bring together archival records, museum artefacts and printed sources to illustrate the history of Powys throughout the Victorian period. Visit this graphic page in our section on Workhouses at http://history.powys.org.uk/history/common/workhse1.html for an example of what we are trying to achieve.

With very little on published on Victorian Wales for Key Stage 2, and nothing on Victorian Powys, the project is breaking new ground, uncovering new stories and bringing them into the classroom via the Internet. For an example of the way national events impacted on local communities, see new evidence of the effects of the Irish potato famine in Victorian Powys at http://history.powys.org.uk/school1/machynlleth/irish2.html.
