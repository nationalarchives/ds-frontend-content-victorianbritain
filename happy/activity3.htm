3. You now have to decide which of the statements below can be used to help explain how the railways changed the lives of Victorian people. Click on the statements that you think are true and then write in the number of the sources that you think back up your choice. Check that you will be able to provide evidence from the sources. Think about all of the sources and what you have learnt from the earlier activities.

[N.B. The statements that can be supported with evidence have the numbers of the appropriate sources. The other statements have an explanation of why they are incorrect.]

A. All Victorians could travel to foreign countries. Although source 5 shows that Victorians had the opportunity to travel abroad, the clothes of the people in the poster give the impression that only well off Victorians could afford these trips.

B. The railway companies encouraged people to visit different places. Sources 2 and 3

C. Railways meant that Thomas Cook could run his excursions. Source 5

D. Sport was popular in Victorian Britain. Sources 1, 2, 6, 7 and 8

E. Travelling to actually watch sport was the only way to find out about it.

Although source 6 shows that large numbers of people watched football matches, Activity 1 mentions another way through which people found out about sport; the newspapers. Railways did mean that newspapers were able to be distributed quicker this meant you could find out the results of sporting fixtures and events much quicker too.
